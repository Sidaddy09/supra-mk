History of Toyota Supra

Toyota Supra MK4

The Toyota Supra MK4, also known as the A80 Supra, was produced from 1993 to 2002. It is one of the most iconic sports cars ever built. The MK4 Supra was powered by a 2JZ-GTE inline-six engine, which is renowned for its ability to handle high levels of modification and produce significant horsepower. It gained fame in the "The Fast and the Furious" movie franchise and is still a sought-after car by enthusiasts worldwide.

The stock top speed of the Toyota Supra MK4 is electronically limited to around 155 mph (250 km/h), and if you modify it, it can go around 350km an hour with double turbo and a wing.

Toyota Supra MK5

The Toyota Supra MK5, also known as the A90 Supra, made a comeback in 2019 after a long hiatus. It was developed in collaboration with BMW and shares some components with the BMW Z4. The MK5 Supra is powered by a turbocharged inline-six engine and has modern features and performance enhancements. It has received both praise and criticism for its departure from the previous Supra generations, but it continues to be a symbol of Toyota's sports car heritage.

The stock top speed of the Toyota Supra MK5 is electronically limited to around 155 mph (250 km/h) and if you modify it too it can go around 400km/h, and that's stunning.

Toyota Supra MK1, MK2, and MK3

Toyota Supra MK1

The MK1 Supra, produced in the late '70s, marked the beginning of the Supra series. It featured a stylish design and a range of engine options, setting the stage for future generations.

Toyota Supra MK2

The MK2 Supra, produced in the early '80s, continued the legacy with enhanced performance and styling. It became known for its sporty appeal and improved driving dynamics.

Toyota Supra MK3

The MK3 Supra, produced in the late '80s to early '90s, further evolved in terms of performance and technology. It featured advanced electronics and a more aerodynamic design.
